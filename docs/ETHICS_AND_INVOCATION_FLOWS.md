# Ethics and Invocation Flows

ETHRAEON systems are invoked ethically — every system entry point is governed by constitutional constraints.

## What This Means

Ethics flows are NOT a separate moral layer bolted on afterward. They are the architectural entry point for each system's invocation.

When you invoke a ETHRAEON system, constitutional constraints are evaluated first:
1. Is the invocation within the system's authorized scope?
2. Is the request within governance boundaries?
3. Is the human operator's authority preserved?
4. Is the audit trail active?

## Constitutional Constraints (T5-Rigid)

T5-Rigid means no amount of prompt engineering can override the constitutional constraints. The system cannot be asked to:
- Ignore governance boundaries
- Bypass human oversight
- Generate unethical outputs

## Governance vs. Capability

Governance is the foundational architecture. Capability is what's built on top. ETHRAEON does not restrict capability — it governs how capability is invoked, evidenced, and controlled.

This means more capability is unlocked with governance, not less.
